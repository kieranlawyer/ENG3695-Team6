The modem and router are two very distinct, but just as integral pieces of equipment to setting up internet in your home. The modem is what connects to your Internet Service Provider (ISP) to your home, the router is what actually distributes this connection to your devices.

There are some tips you can employ to try and optimize performance within your house. Try to place the router in a central location, and try not to have anything interrupting or interferring with the signal in between it and whatever it is connecting to. NAT stands for Network Address Translation and it is a process used by routers to manage multiple devices by applying a public IP address to all of them. If it is open, the router is not blocking any traffic. This is best for things like gaming and streaming. If it is moderate, it is filtering some traffic, but most is going through. It is a balance between open and the next type, strict. Strict is when your router is filtering traffic heavily, which may lead to issues with things like gaming, but will keep you more secure. If you are having issues with your NAT type, try to enable Universal Plug and Play (UPnP), as this should help tune your network to what you are currently doing.
