User Guide

This section provides step-by-step instructions to help you quickly and easily set up your Dbit N300 router. Whether you're a first-time user or configuring advanced settings, you'll find clear guidance to get your network up and running.


In this guide, you'll learn how to:

  • Unbox and connect your router
  • Connect to the Wi-Fi network
  • Access the router's web interface
  • Configure Wi-Fi settings and network security
  • Secure the router control panel
  • (Optional) Configure QoS

Dont feel like reading? No worries! Check out this quick video describing the setup and the benefits of the Dbit N300 Router!

Follow the instructions carefully, and if you encounter any issues, check the Troubleshooting or FAQ sections for additional help.

1. Unbox and connect your router


In the box, you should have:

  1. The router
  2. The power cable
  3. An ethernet cable (RJ45)

Remove all items from the box. Unwrap them and place them aside.

The contents of the box.

On the back of the N300 you will find a few different ports:

The ports on the back of the router.

Connect the power cord to a source of power, then connect the other side of the cable to the port on the back of the routed labeled DC IN. You should now see a RED light on the top of the router.


Connect one side of the RJ45 ethernet cable to the port labeled WAN. Connect the other side of the ethernet cable to a LAN port on your modem.


(Optionally, you may connect the other side to a LAN port on existing router instead of a modem. This will turn your Dbit N300 router into a secondary access point to the same network. Moving the Dbit N300 to the other side of your house from the first router will allow for enhanced connectivity for devices that were previously far from the router.)


If all has gone well, the light on the top of the router should turn from RED to GREEN.


Note: It may take a few minutes to change color. You may proceed to step 2 as long as the light is lit up, regardless of color.

2. Connect to the Wi-Fi network


Pick up a device capable of connecting to Wi-Fi. Your phone or laptop will work.


Head to the Wi-Fi settings and look for a network called N300-36E1.

N300 Wi-Fi network

Connect to this network. The default password is 123456789. If this password does NOT work, hold the button labeled RESET for ten seconds. Wait one minute, then re-try.


If everything in step 1 went well and you have a GREEN light on top of your router, you should have access to the internet via this network!


Unfortunately, your network is UNSECURE at this point and needs further configuration to be safe to use. Continue to step 3 for futher setup!

3. Access the router's web interface


First, open the web browser using the device you connected to the N300 network on.

Navigate to the url http://192.168.10.1/. You should be greeted with the interface shown below:

The login page

The default login credentials are:

Username: admin

Password: admin


If this login information does NOT work, hold the button labeled RESET on the back of the router for ten seconds. Wait one minute, then repeat step 2 to re-connect to the network, and repeat step 3 up to here.


Upon logging in, you should see something like the following:

Control panel landing page

Now it's time to configure your router to your liking! Move on to step 4.

4. Configure Wi-Fi settings and network security


Once connected and logged into the web interface, head to the WIFI section using the navigation panel on the left side. Here you will need to set up the SSID and the Password.

SSID and password configuration

The SSID is the name of the network. This is what appears in the WiFi list on your devices.

The password is required to connect to the network. This is not associated with the login to the control panel.


You may pick any SSID you find suitable, so long as it is no longer than 32 characters and does not contain illegal characters.


It is recommended to pick a secure password. As a general rule of thumb:

  1. Make them long
  2. Make them random
  3. Make them unique

Your networks security is dependent on a secure choice of password. If your password is too weak, attackers may join your network and monitor activity. They may be able to extract login credentials or access tokens from websites you visit. It is imperative you pick a secure password.


For more information on picking a secure password, please refer to this resource.


Once you have updated the SSID and password, click the Save button at the bottom center of the page.


NOTE: Once you save the settings, you will be kicked from the network if the SSID has changed. You must head back to step 2 substituting N300-36E1 for the new SSID you have picked to re-connect.


Congratulations, you now have customized your network name and secured your network from outside attackers!


Unfortunately, the router control panel is still easily accessible from anyone connected to the network. Further setup is required to secure the control panel. Head to step 5 for futher setup.

5. Secure the router control panel


The final step of necessary setup is to secure the control panel.


Navigate to the System page using the navigation menu on the left. At the top of the page, you should see the following configuration box:

Control panel login configuration

Type the old password (by default admin) into the Old password box.


Pick a new password. Refer back to step 4 for information on picking a secure password. It is recommended to save this password somewhere safe - either physically write it on a piece of paper and store it in a safe place, or use a password manager.


The password picked must be between 8-30 characters and must contain 1 uppercase letter, 1 lowercase letter, and 1 number. It can NOT contain special characters.


Once you have picked a new password and securely saved it down, type it into both the New password and Confirm password boxes.


Finally, click the Save button at the bottom center of the page.


You will be sent back to the login page. Login with your new credentials to ensure you still have access.


If you find yourself unable to login to the control panel, you may Factory reset your router to restore default configuration. To do so, hold the RESET button on the back of the router down for 10 seconds. Wait one minute, then you must repeat the setup instructions starting from step 2.



Congratulations! Your router is now set up and ready to use. You may now connect up your devices around the house and access the internet.


Some additional configuration options will be discussed below. The remaining setup steps are optional.

6. (Optional) Configure QoS


Quality of Service (QoS) allows you to prioritize network traffic to ensure that critical applications receive sufficient bandwidth. This is especially useful for reducing lag in online gaming, ensuring smooth video streaming, and preventing slowdowns during video calls.


In this section, you may configure bandwidth limits per each device connected to the network. You may also temporarily disable certain devices from accessing the internet. These devices remain connected to your router but are no longer sucking up precious bandwidth. Disabled devices will be temporarily moved to the "Prohibited devices" section. You may delete them from this section to re-enable their internet connection.


Warning: disabling internet for the device you are using to acccess the router page will prevent you from being able to access the router page to re-enable the connection. If this happens, you must connect from a new device, log in, and remove your devices from the "Prohibited devices" section.

The QoS Section

After any changes, the Save button at the bottom center of the page must be clicked.

Conclusion


Congratulations! You should now have your Dbit N300 router working and configured to your liking. Your internet connection should be secure, stable, and tailored to your needs.


If you encountered any issues, be sure to check out the Troubleshooting and FAQ sections for quick solutions. For additional support, visit the Contact page to find additional support resources and to reach out for further assistance.


To keep your network running smoothly, consider periodically checking for firmware updates, reviewing security settings, and optimizing your Wi-Fi placement for the best coverage.
