User Guide

This section provides step-by-step instructions to help you quickly and easily set up your Dbit N300 router. Whether you're a first-time user or configuring advanced settings, you'll find clear guidance to get your network up and running.


In this guide, you'll learn how to:

  • ✔ Unbox and connect your router
  • ✔ Access the router's web interface
  • ✔ Configure Wi-Fi settings and security
  • ✔ Set up parental controls
  • ✔ Configure metering/bandwidth limits
  • ✔ Create guest networks
  • ✔ Update firmware for optimal performance

Follow the instructions carefully, and if you encounter any issues, check the Troubleshooting or FAQ sections for additional help.

1. Unbox and connect your router


On the back of the N300 you will find a few different ports, and in your box it should have an ethernet cord and a power cord. Connect the power cord to a source of power, and the port on the far right side. On the port directly to the left, connect one side of the ethernet cable, and the other goes into your modem.

2. Access the router's web interface


To log into the router itself you will need to go to a search engine and enter the following IP address: 192.168.10.1. There is a default login on the bottom of your router, or you may need to enter your selected username and password if you changed them.

3. Configure Wi-Fi settings and security


Once connected and logged into the web interface, head to the internet section on the left side, and select ‘DHCP’. Then, go to down to the wifi section, and setup your SSID and Password. The SSID is simply the name for your wifi, and your password will help you keep your network secure.

4. Set up parental controls


Under the 'QoS' tab, you are able to check what devices are connected to your network and limit their internet access altogether. Turn off the internet access for the gaming console, tablets, computers, or anything else your kids have connected to wifi, whenever you need to.

6. Configure metering/bandwidth limits


In the same tab as above, the 'QoS' tab, you are able to set up an upload and download speed limit to ensure no one device is hogging all of the network.

8. Create guest networks


Under the 'WiFi' tab, you are able to enable a guest network, set it's name and password, and toggle it on or off. Use this to allow others to connect to your network without compromising the security of your network.

9. Update firmware for optimal performance


Under the 'System' tab, you are able to check for updates, and update if there is one available. It is best practice to check for these updates at least once a month, as they often include valuable security patches and performance improvements that helps keep your network running safely and quickly. Note: Your network will be down during the update period, but should automatically reconnect to the modem, and your devices in turn.

Conclusion


Congratulations! You should now have your Dbit N300 router working and configured to your liking. Your internet connection should be secure, stable, and tailored to your needs.


If you encountered any issues, be sure to check out the Troubleshooting and FAQ sections for quick solutions. For additional support, visit the Contact page to find additional support resources and to reach out for further assistance.


To keep your network running smoothly, consider periodically checking for firmware updates, reviewing security settings, and optimizing your Wi-Fi placement for the best coverage.
